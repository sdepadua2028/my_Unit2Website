Main Dishes


Sinigang

Sinigang is a sour soup made with tamarind-flavored broth and a number of vegetables, including string beans, spinach, okra, tomatoes, and daikon radish. It can be made with both meat and seafood, such as pork, beef, fish, or shrimp.

(Photo from "Sinigang na Baboy" by Kawaling Pinoy)

Lechon Kawali

"Lechon" refers to a whole suckling pig, while "kawali" refers to the pan used to make this delicious, crispy, fried pork belly. Before the pork is fried, it is boiled with garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns to give it a distinct aroma and flavor. Lechon kawali is often served with a sweet and savory sauce or lechon sauce (a Filipino condiment).

(Photo from "Lechon Kawali" by cooking.nytimes.com )

Adobo

Also known as the unofficial national dish of the Phillipines, adobo is a stew made with chicken or pork marinated in soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic. To add more flavor, it is topped with peppercorns and basil leaves.
