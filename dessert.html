Desserts


Halo-Halo

"Halo-halo" literally translates to "mix-mix", named for its many components and toppings, which are all stirred together right before eating. The most main ingredients are shaved ice, milk, and ice cream. More popular ingredients are listed below:

  • Fruits (ex. jackfruit)
  • Jellies (ex. cocnut gel)
  • Purple Yam Jam
  • Leche Flan
  • Shaved Coconut
  • Tapioca Pearls
  • Sweetened Beans (ex. mung beans)
  • Crispy Rice/Corn Flakes
(Photo from "Halo-Halo" by Tiana Gee-bonappétit.com)

Leche Flan

Leche flan is a custard made from egg yolks, condensed and evaporated milk, and caramel. It is ususally steamed in a round tin pan called a "llanera", which makes it more rich and creamy. As mentioned above, it is a popular topping for halo-halo.

(Photo from "Leche Flan with Whole Eggs" by Kawaling Pinoy)

Bibingka

Bibingka is a rice cake made from key ingredients including ground rice (galapong), melted butter, and coconut milk. Traditionally, it is topped with salted eggs, but is more commonly sprinkled with shaved coconut and cheese. Like leche flan, bibingka is steamed, but is lined with banana leaves instead, giving it its well-known aroma.
